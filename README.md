## VITyarthi-Digital Literacy Project
### This project is designed to develop essential digital literacy skills by engaging students in a series of practical, real-world tasks. As a Student Digital Ambassador, the project focuses on understanding how to use digital tools effectively, communicate professionally, and maintain a responsible online presence. It covers multiple aspects such as creating a digital awareness infographic, building a professional portfolio on platforms like GitHub and LinkedIn, exploring coding and collaboration tools, practicing email etiquette, and understanding cybercrime and online safety.
### The overall aim of the project is to bridge the gap between basic digital usage and responsible digital behavior. It emphasizes not only technical skills but also awareness, professionalism, and critical thinking while using digital platforms. By completing these tasks, students gain hands-on experience that prepares them for academic success and future career opportunities, while also enabling them to guide their peers in making smarter and safer digital choices.

## MODULE 1:
### This task involved creating a one-page digital literacy infographic using Canva. The design explained the meaning of digital literacy and highlighted key areas such as safe internet practices and maintaining a professional online presence. It focused on important habits like using strong passwords, avoiding suspicious links, and building a clean digital footprint. The use of icons and color-coded sections made the information clear and easy to understand. Overall, the task showed how visual design can simplify complex ideas, although fitting all content into a single page required careful organization.

## MODULE 2:
### This task involved creating and updating profiles on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Kaggle to build a professional digital presence. GitHub was used to showcase coding projects and create a profile README, LinkedIn to establish a professional identity and network, and Kaggle to practice data science skills. Together, these platforms support learning, collaboration, and career growth. Over the next four years, the plan is to actively use them for project development, networking, and skill improvement to build a strong and well-rounded portfolio.

## MODULE 3:
### This task involved using HackerRank for coding practice and Google Forms for collaboration. A beginner-level problem on HackerRank helped build basic problem-solving and coding skills. Additionally, a Digital Literacy Awareness Quiz was created using Google Forms and linked to Google Sheets for easy response collection and analysis. These tools support both technical skill development and effective collaboration, making them valuable for academic work and future career preparation.

## MODULE 4:
### Poor digital communication, such as sending unprofessional or unclear emails, can lead to misunderstandings, delayed responses, or being ignored. This can be avoided by following proper email etiquette, including using a clear subject line, polite tone, and well-structured message. Good communication helps create a positive impression and ensures clarity.

## MODULE 5:
### The research showed that cybercrimes like UPI and phishing scams are often simple but highly convincing, relying on psychological tricks rather than advanced technology. It highlighted that many victims are unaware rather than careless. As a result, the key takeaway is to be more cautious—avoid suspicious links, verify payment details, and never share sensitive information like OTPs or PINs to stay safe online.
